In Brief

The Easy Way to Understand It

One page, plain language: why this is needed, how the chain of responsibility works, how a task gets created and tracked, what the safety rules and punishments look like, and how a Minister actually loses office. The formal legal text, clause by clause, is in The Bill below.

Note on this document. This is a citizen‑drafted concept note prepared for public debate and advocacy, in the style of a legislative Bill. It is not an official Bill introduced in Parliament, has not been vetted by the Ministry of Law & Justice or Legislative Department, and would require formal Cabinet approval, pre‑legislative consultation, and drafting by the Legislative Counsel before it could be tabled.
🩸 Named in memory of Shaheed Bhagat Singh (1907–1931), who held the colonial system to account when it hid behind procedure and anonymity. This Bill borrows his refusal to let power dissolve into "the system" — every duty here is fixed to a name, a post, and a deadline.

Why BHAGAT?

🗣️ Right now, when something goes wrong, everyone can blame everyone else — and often no one is punished. BHAGAT writes down, in advance and in public, exactly which 4 people are responsible for every task.
  • Ministers answer to Parliament as a whole (Art. 75(3)/164(2)) — not built for tracing one failed task.
  • India's existing graft watchdog and information‑request law are both reactive — a citizen must ask first.
  • BHAGAT fixes 4 named roles to every task, before it even starts.
  • Every task is tagged Governance, Administration, or Transparency — so you can ask the right question.

Old model

One diffuse "department." Everyone points at everyone else. Punishment, if any, lands on the most junior name — or no one.

BHAGAT model

4 named roles per task, each with a public record on bhagat.nic.in. Failure is traced to the exact duty that wasn't performed.

How a Task Gets Created & Tracked

🗣️ Every task lives inside a government File. The moment that File opens, it must be registered on the Portal with a Task ID and named officers — before work begins, not after something goes wrong.
  • Opening a File on a qualifying matter creates the Task and starts the clock.
  • Named officers, budget, deadline and milestones go on record on day one.
  • A durable site board + QR code is put up at the actual worksite.
  • On the Portal: search by ID, QR, map or category — budget tracker, grievance tracker, safety‑certificate archive, and Ministerial scorecard, all in one place.

How a Minister Actually Leaves Office

🗣️ Only the PM/CM can effectively remove a Minister — no law can change that. BHAGAT keeps a public scorecard instead, forcing that choice into daylight.

Step 2

Public Hearing

→
Step 3

Forced Political Choice

Record goes to PM/CM & Speaker — 30 days to remove, or publicly justify keeping the Minister.

Chapter I

Preliminary

Short title, extent and commencement.1. (1) This Act may be called the Basic Hierarchical Accountability for Governance, Administration and Transparency Act, 2026.
(2) It extends to the whole of India.
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint, and different dates may be appointed for different provisions of this Act.

Definitions.2. In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires,— (a) "Public Task" means any function, project, scheme, examination, investigation, service or duty performed by or on behalf of the Government which has a defined outcome, a defined timeline, and a specific public consequence if not performed or performed defectively; (b) "Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain" or "HTAC" means the four‑tier chain of responsibility for a Public Task, comprising the Executive Officer, the Reviewing Officer, the Administrative Head and the Political Head; (c) "Executive Officer" means the officer directly and physically responsible for executing a Public Task; (d) "Reviewing Officer" means the immediate supervisory officer responsible for oversight, resource provision and periodic review of a Public Task; (e) "Administrative Head" means the departmental or district head responsible for the systemic and procedural adequacy of a Public Task; (f) "Political Head" means the Minister‑in‑charge responsible, before Parliament or the State Legislature and before the public, for the policy and budgetary decisions governing a Public Task; (g) "Accountability Officer" means a District, State or Central Accountability Officer appointed under section 18; (h) "Technical Member" means a subject‑matter expert attached to an Accountability Officer under section 20; (i) "Portal" means the public digital platform established under section 8 and known as bhagat.nic.in; (j) "Governance Pillar" means that aspect of a Public Task concerning the policy, budgetary or design decision to undertake it; (k) "Administration Pillar" means that aspect of a Public Task concerning its execution and supervision on the ground; (l) "Transparency Pillar" means that aspect of a Public Task concerning its disclosure, registration and reporting to the public; (m) "appropriate Government" means the Central Government in relation to a Public Task of the Union and a State Government in relation to a Public Task of that State; (n) "competent Government authority" means the authority empowered under the Constitution or applicable service rules to take disciplinary action against the officer or Minister concerned; (o) "prescribed" means prescribed by rules made under this Act.

— 3 —

Chapter II

The Task Accountability Register and Obligations of Public Authorities

Registration of Public Tasks and the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain.3. (1) Every Public Task meeting such threshold of public impact as may be prescribed shall, within thirty days of being sanctioned, ordered or otherwise authorised, be registered on the Portal with a unique identification number, and in any case before it commences.
(2) Every registration under sub‑section (1) shall name, by post and by officer where known, the Executive Officer, the Reviewing Officer, the Administrative Head and the Political Head constituting the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain for that Task, together with the timeline and outcome by which its performance shall be judged.
(3) Where a Public Task is a criminal investigation, the Executive Officer shall be the Investigating or Circle Officer, the Reviewing Officer shall be the Superintendent of Police or equivalent, and the statutory target for identification of an accused shall be thirty days from registration of the first information report, subject to such extension as may be recorded and published on the Portal with reasons.
(4) Failure to register a Public Task within the period specified in sub‑section (1) shall be deemed, for the purposes of Chapter IV, to be a default of the Reviewing Officer.

The four tiers of accountability.4. The Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain of every Public Task shall comprise, and no more than, the following four tiers: the Executive Officer, the Reviewing Officer, the Administrative Head, and the Political Head, each independently liable only for the duty assigned to that tier under section 3.

Liability confined to own duty.5. (1) No officer or Political Head in the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain of a Public Task shall be liable under this Act for a failure that is not attributable to the duty specifically assigned to that officer or Political Head under section 3.
(2) More than one tier of the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain may be found independently liable in respect of the same Public Task where each has failed a distinct duty assigned to it.

Display of Task information at site and interoperability of the Register.6. (1) Information identifying the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain of a Public Task involving physical infrastructure shall be displayed at the site of execution in such form as may be prescribed.
(2) The Register maintained under this Chapter shall, to the extent prescribed, be made interoperable with existing public procurement, works‑monitoring and grievance‑redress systems of the appropriate Government, so as to avoid duplicate reporting.

— 4 —

Obligations of public authorities — proactive disclosure.7. (1) Every public authority responsible for a Public Task shall proactively disclose on the Portal, without requiring a citizen to ask for it,— (a) the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain, and the post and, where known, the name of every officer and Political Head constituting it; (b) the category and Pillar tagging of the Task under sections 10 and 11; (c) the sanctioned cost, sanctioned timeline and milestones of the Task, and the actual cost and actual timeline as they become known; (d) every due diligence certificate and dry‑run certificate issued under sections 12 and 13; (e) the procedure for filing a grievance under section 9, and the name and contact particulars of the Reviewing Officer to whom it may in the first instance be addressed; (f) every finding of an Own‑Duty Failure, and every penalty imposed or recommended, in respect of the Task; and (g) such other particulars as may be prescribed, and shall thereafter keep such disclosure updated at such intervals as may be prescribed.
(2) It shall be the constant endeavour of every public authority to publish the particulars in sub‑section (1) suo motu and to keep such publication current, so that the public have minimum need to resort to filing a grievance under section 9 to obtain such particulars.

Establishment and functions of the Portal.8. (1) The Central Government shall establish and maintain a public digital platform, to be known as bhagat.nic.in, for the purposes of this Act.
(2) The Portal shall provide, at minimum, a searchable Task Accountability Register, a dry‑run and due‑diligence certificate archive, a fatality and injury protocol tracker, a grievance‑filing facility, and a public Ministerial Accountability Record.

Grievance filing and disposal through the Portal.9. (1) Any person may file a grievance in respect of a registered Public Task through the Portal.
(2) Every grievance filed under sub‑section (1) shall be disposed of by the Reviewing Officer as expeditiously as possible, and in any case within thirty days of its receipt, or within such extended period, not exceeding forty‑five days in aggregate, as the Reviewing Officer may, for reasons to be recorded in writing, require.
(3) Where a grievance is not disposed of within the period specified in sub‑section (2), it shall be deemed to have been referred to the Accountability Officer having jurisdiction, as if it were a second appeal under section 25.
(4) Every grievance and its disposal shall be tracked and published on the Portal in the manner prescribed.

— 5 —

Classification of Public Tasks into categories.10. Every Public Task shall, at the time of registration under section 3, be classified into one of the following categories:— (a) Category A — Infrastructure and Construction; (b) Category B — Public Events and Mass Gatherings; (c) Category C — Public Examinations; (d) Category D — Regulatory Clearances; (e) Category E — Investigations; (f) Category F — Recurring Public Services.

Pillar tagging.11. Every Public Task shall, in addition to its category under section 10, be tagged on the Register with the Governance Pillar, the Administration Pillar, or the Transparency Pillar, or any combination thereof, that it engages, and such tagging shall be searchable by the public on the Portal.

Due diligence certification.12. No Public Task falling under Category A or Category D shall be marked as under execution on the Register unless the Executive Officer has certified, and the Reviewing Officer has independently verified, completion of all due diligence applicable to that Task, including, as applicable, an approved detailed project report, structural safety certificate, environmental clearance and land‑title verification.

Mandatory dry run before commissioning.13. (1) No Public Task falling under Category A involving new public infrastructure shall be opened for public use, no Public Task falling under Category B shall be conducted, and no Public Task falling under Category C shall be administered, unless a dry run has first been completed and certified by a Safety Certifying Officer, being a Technical Member independent of the Executive Officer.
(2) A certificate issued under sub‑section (1) shall be published on the Portal, and any deficiency identified during the dry run shall be rectified and re‑verified before the Task proceeds.

Consequence of non-compliance with sections 12 and 13.14. Commissioning, opening or conducting a Public Task referred to in section 12 or section 13 without the certification required thereunder, or the issuance of a false certificate, shall by itself constitute an Extreme‑category Own‑Duty Failure under Chapter IV, irrespective of whether any harm results therefrom.

Protocol on death or grievous hurt.15. (1) Where a person dies or suffers grievous hurt arising from the execution or failure of a Public Task, the following shall take effect immediately, and without awaiting the outcome of any inquiry:— (a) the event shall be treated, on a provisional basis, as an Extreme‑category matter for the purposes of this Act; (b) the Executive Officer and Reviewing Officer shall stand suspended pending inquiry; (c) an information report shall be registered wherever the facts disclose a cognizable offence; (d) a Compensation Disbursal Officer, designated in advance for every Category A and Category B Task, shall release interim compensation to the victim or next of kin within fifteen days of the event, irrespective of the outcome of any inquiry into fault, and without prejudice to any further legal remedy.
(2) Whether, and by which tier of the Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain, an Own‑Duty Failure was in fact committed shall be determined only by inquiry under Chapter IV; nothing in sub‑section (1) shall be treated as a finding of fault against any person.
(3) Where such inquiry finds that certification under section 12 or section 13 was skipped, delayed or falsely made, that finding shall by itself be sufficient for dismissal of the certifying officer.

— 6 —

Recording of budget and time overruns.16. Where the actual cost or time taken for a Public Task exceeds its sanctioned cost or timeline, the Administrative Head shall record the cause of such overrun on the Register within fifteen days of it becoming known.

Escalation of policy-caused overruns to the political head.17. Where an overrun recorded under section 16 is found to be caused by a policy change, a scope change ordered by the appropriate Government, or a delay attributable to a governmental decision, the Political Head shall, within thirty sitting days of such finding, table a Budget Overrun Justification before the House and cause it to be published on the Portal.

— 7 —

Chapter III

The Accountability Officers

District, State and Central Accountability Officers.18. There shall be constituted, for the purposes of this Act,— (a) a District Accountability Officer for every district; (b) a State Accountability Officer for every State and Union territory; and (c) a Central Accountability Officer for the Union, each of whom shall be a single officer exercising the powers conferred by this Chapter and Chapter IV.

Qualifications, rank, independence and oath of office.19. (1) The District Accountability Officer shall be an officer not below the rank of a District Judge, and shall be liable to removal only in the manner, and before the authority, applicable to a District Judge.
(2) The State Accountability Officer shall be a sitting or retired Judge of a High Court, and shall be liable to removal only in the manner applicable to a Judge of a High Court.
(3) The Central Accountability Officer shall be a sitting or retired Judge of the Supreme Court, or a former Chief Justice of a High Court, and shall be liable to removal only in the manner applicable to a Judge of the Supreme Court.
(4) No Accountability Officer shall, during the tenure of that office, be subordinate to the Administrative Head or Political Head of any Public Task under inquiry before that Officer.
(5) Every Accountability Officer shall, before entering upon office under this Chapter, make and subscribe, before such authority as may be prescribed, an oath or affirmation in the form set out in the First Schedule.

Technical Members.20. An Accountability Officer may, for the purposes of an inquiry under Chapter IV, associate with such Technical Members possessing domain expertise relevant to the Public Task under inquiry as may be prescribed.

— 8 —

Chapter IV

Powers and Functions of the Accountability Officers, Appeal and Penalties

Jurisdiction, powers of inquiry, and reference of corruption matters.21. (1) A District Accountability Officer shall have jurisdiction over Public Tasks executed within that district, a State Accountability Officer over Public Tasks of that State, and a Central Accountability Officer over Public Tasks of the Union and over appeals referred to it under this Chapter.
(2) For the purposes of an inquiry under this Chapter, an Accountability Officer shall have the powers of a civil court under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, in respect of summoning and enforcing the attendance of witnesses and the discovery and production of documents, but shall not otherwise exercise any power of search or seizure.
(3) Where an inquiry under this Chapter discloses facts constituting an offence under any law relating to corruption by public servants, the Accountability Officer shall refer the matter to the authority competent to investigate that offence under such law, without prejudice to the continuation of the inquiry under this Act.

Reasoned recommendation to the competent Government authority.22. On conclusion of an inquiry under this Chapter, the Accountability Officer shall record a reasoned recommendation and transmit it to the competent Government authority and, simultaneously, to the Portal for publication.

Duty of Government on receipt of a recommendation.23. The competent Government authority receiving a recommendation under section 22 shall, within thirty days, either act upon it or publish on the Portal its reasons for not acting upon it in the manner recommended; nothing in this section shall be construed as conferring upon an Accountability Officer the power to itself dismiss, remove or otherwise punish any officer or Political Head.

Departmental appeal.24. (1) A person aggrieved by a finding recorded against an officer under this Act, or by the disposal of a grievance under section 9, may, within thirty days of such finding or disposal, prefer an appeal to the departmental authority senior in rank to the Reviewing Officer concerned:
Provided that such authority may admit the appeal after the expiry of the said period if satisfied that the appellant was prevented by sufficient cause from filing it in time.
(2) An appeal under sub‑section (1) shall be disposed of within thirty days of its receipt, or within such extended period, not exceeding forty‑five days in aggregate, as may, for reasons to be recorded in writing, be required.

Second appeal to the Accountability Officer.25. (1) A person aggrieved by an order passed in appeal under section 24, or by the expiry of the period specified therein without an order being passed, may, within ninety days of such order or expiry, prefer a second appeal to the District, State or Central Accountability Officer having jurisdiction.
(2) The Accountability Officer shall dispose of a second appeal under this section, and record a recommendation under section 22, within one hundred and eighty days of its receipt.
(3) The onus of proving that a finding or action under appeal was justified shall lie on the officer whose finding or action is under appeal.

— 9 —

Graded schedule of punishments.26. The competent Government authority, on a recommendation under section 22 or section 25, may impose, according to the gravity of the Own‑Duty Failure and consistent with applicable service rules, penalties ranging from a recorded warning, withholding of increment, and adverse entry, to transfer, reduction in rank, compulsory retirement, and dismissal from service.

Daily penalty for delay or default.27. (1) Where the Accountability Officer, while disposing of a second appeal under section 25, is of the opinion that the Executive Officer or the Reviewing Officer concerned has, without reasonable cause, failed to register a Public Task within the time specified in section 3, failed to dispose of a grievance within the time specified in section 9, or knowingly caused incorrect, incomplete or misleading particulars to be entered on the Register, the Accountability Officer shall, as part of the recommendation under section 22, recommend imposition of a penalty of two hundred and fifty rupees for every day of such default, so however that the total penalty recommended in respect of one default shall not exceed twenty‑five thousand rupees.
(2) No penalty shall be recommended under sub‑section (1) without giving the officer concerned a reasonable opportunity of being heard, and the burden of proving that the officer acted reasonably and diligently shall lie on that officer.
(3) Where a default referred to in sub‑section (1) is found to be persistent, the Accountability Officer shall, in addition to the penalty under sub‑section (1), recommend that disciplinary action be taken against the officer under the service rules applicable to him.

Statement of Task Failure before the House.28. Where an Extreme‑category Own‑Duty Failure is recorded in respect of a Public Task, the Political Head shall table a Statement of Task Failure before the House, and cause it to be published on the Portal, within fifteen sitting days of the finding.

— 10 —

Chapter V

Removal of a Political Head from Office

Ministerial Accountability Record.29. The Portal shall maintain, in respect of every Political Head, a public and cumulative Ministerial Accountability Record of every Extreme‑category Own‑Duty Failure, Budget Overrun Justification and Statement of Task Failure attributed to that Political Head under this Act.

Mandatory review hearing.30. On the third entry, or an earlier entry involving loss of life, recorded against a Political Head in the Ministerial Accountability Record, the State or Central Accountability Officer, as the case may be, shall hold a public review hearing and record its findings thereon.

Transmission to the House and the Head of Government; saving of constitutional procedure.31. (1) The findings of a review hearing under section 30 shall be transmitted to the Speaker of the House concerned and to the Prime Minister or, as the case may be, the Chief Minister.
(2) Nothing in this Chapter shall be construed as affecting the operation of article 75 or article 164 of the Constitution, and the continuance in office of a Political Head shall remain a matter for the President or the Governor, as the case may be, acting in accordance with the Constitution.

— 11 —

Chapter VI

Miscellaneous

Protection of persons acting in good faith.32. No suit, prosecution or other legal proceeding shall lie against any person for anything done or intended to be done in good faith under this Act, including the filing of a grievance or complaint disclosed to be made in good faith, and such person shall be entitled to such protection against victimisation as may be prescribed.

Penalty for false or frivolous complaints.33. Whoever files a complaint under this Act knowing it to be false or frivolous shall be liable to such penalty as may be prescribed.

Act to have overriding effect.34. The provisions of this Act relating to the registration and public disclosure of a Public Task shall have effect notwithstanding anything inconsistent therewith contained in any departmental manual, service regulation, standing order or instruction for the time being in force.

Relationship with other laws.35. The provisions of this Act shall be in addition to, and not in derogation of, any other law for the time being in force relating to corruption by public servants, disclosure of information by public authorities, or the conduct and discipline of public servants.

Ministerial responsibility to the House not affected.36. Nothing in this Act shall derogate from the collective responsibility of the Council of Ministers to the House under article 75(3) or article 164(2) of the Constitution.

Independence of the Election Commission preserved.37. Nothing in this Act shall apply so as to attach a Political Head to a Public Task relating to the conduct of elections, which shall remain subject to the exclusive superintendence, direction and control of the Election Commission under article 324 of the Constitution.

— 12 —

Act not to require public disclosure in relation to certain organisations.38. (1) Nothing in this Act shall require public disclosure on the Portal of particulars of a Public Task of an intelligence or security organisation specified in the Second Schedule, being an organisation established by the Central Government, or of any information furnished by such organisation to that Government:
Provided that a Public Task of such organisation shall nonetheless be registered, and an internal Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain maintained, in the manner prescribed, for the purposes of inquiry under Chapter IV:
Provided further that this sub‑section shall not exclude from public disclosure any Own‑Duty Failure involving an allegation of corruption or of violation of human rights, which shall be inquired into and disclosed in the manner prescribed after such security review as may be necessary.
(2) The Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, amend the Second Schedule so as to include or omit an organisation, and every such notification shall be laid before each House of Parliament.
(3) A State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, specify intelligence or security organisations established by that Government to which sub‑section (1) shall apply, subject to the provisos thereto, and every such notification shall be laid before the State Legislature.

Monitoring and annual reporting.39. (1) Every Accountability Officer shall, as soon as practicable after the end of each financial year, prepare a report on the working of this Act within that Officer's jurisdiction during that year, and forward a copy to the appropriate Government and to the Portal for publication.
(2) Every report under sub‑section (1) shall state, in respect of the year to which it relates,— (a) the number of Public Tasks registered, category‑wise; (b) the number of grievances and appeals received and disposed of, and the average time taken; (c) the number of Own‑Duty Failures found, tier‑wise, and the penalties imposed or recommended in respect thereof; (d) the number of Statements of Task Failure tabled under section 28 and of Ministerial Accountability Record entries made under section 29; and (e) recommendations, if any, for the reform of this Act or of the rules made thereunder.
(3) The appropriate Government shall cause a copy of every report received under this section to be laid before the House or the State Legislature, as the case may be.

Power to make rules by appropriate Government.40. (1) The appropriate Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, make rules to carry out the provisions of this Act.
(2) In particular, and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing power, such rules may provide for all or any of the following matters, namely:— (a) the threshold of public impact above which a Public Task must be registered under section 3; (b) the form of display of Task information under section 6; (c) the particulars to be published under section 7, and the manner and intervals of such publication; (d) the fee, if any, and procedure for grievance filing under section 9; (e) the salaries, allowances and conditions of service of Accountability Officers and Technical Members under sections 19 and 20; (f) the manner of recovery of a penalty recommended under section 27; (g) the manner of internal registration of Tasks under section 38; and (h) any other matter which is required to be, or may be, prescribed.

Power to make rules by constitutional authority.41. (1) The Speaker of the House of the People or of the Legislative Assembly of a State, the Chairman of the Council of States or of a Legislative Council, the Chief Justice of India, the Chief Justice of a High Court, the President, the Governor, or the administrator appointed under article 239 of the Constitution, as the case may be, may, in relation to a Public Task of a body under that authority's administrative control, make rules to carry out the provisions of this Act consistently with the autonomy of that body.

Statement of Objects and Reasons

Under article 75(3) of the Constitution, the Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to the House of the People, and under article 164(2), State Councils of Ministers are similarly responsible to their Legislative Assemblies. This model of collective, general responsibility, while constitutionally sound, does not by itself fix, in advance and in public, which specific officer or Minister is responsible for the execution of a given public task.

2. Experience shows that the most common failure in the execution of public tasks is not the absence of any responsible person, but the informal diffusion of responsibility across several levels of the administration, with no single record establishing who was to do what, by when, and with what consequence for failure.

3. This Bill accordingly seeks to establish a four‑tier Hierarchical Task Accountability Chain — an Executive Officer, a Reviewing Officer, an Administrative Head and a Political Head — for every public task of defined public consequence, to be recorded on a public digital register, together with day‑bound proactive disclosure obligations on every public authority, mandatory due diligence and dry‑run requirements for high‑risk categories of task, a fatality and injury protocol, single Accountability Officers at the district, State and central level empowered to recommend action, a two‑stage appeal process with fixed timelines for disposal, a graded schedule of penalties, and a daily monetary penalty for unreasonable delay or default, on the well‑tested pattern already used elsewhere in Indian law for citizen‑facing disclosure and appeal mechanisms, here adapted to the accountability of tasks rather than the disclosure of information.

4. The Bill does not, and constitutionally cannot, confer upon any authority created by it the power to itself remove a Minister from office; it instead creates a public, cumulative Ministerial Accountability Record and a mandatory review hearing, so that the political consequence of repeated or grave task failures is a matter of public record placed before the House, the Prime Minister or Chief Minister, and the electorate.

5. The Bill seeks to achieve the above objects.

Financial Memorandum

Clause 8 of the Bill provides for the establishment and maintenance of a public digital platform (the Portal). Clause 18 provides for the appointment of District, State and Central Accountability Officers, and clause 20 for the association of Technical Members with them. The Bill, if enacted, would involve expenditure from the Consolidated Fund of India and the Consolidated Fund of the States concerned, towards the establishment and maintenance of the Portal, and the salaries, allowances and office expenses of the Accountability Officers and Technical Members and their supporting staff.

2. It is estimated that the Bill would involve a non‑recurring expenditure of approximately ₹250 crore towards the development of the Portal and the initial establishment of the offices of Accountability Officers, and a recurring expenditure of approximately ₹180 crore per annum thereafter. Penalties recovered under clause 27 shall be credited to the Consolidated Fund of India or of the State concerned, as the case may be, and are not expected to be material. No other recurring or non‑recurring expenditure is anticipated.

Memorandum Regarding Delegated Legislation

Sub‑section (1) of section 40 of the Bill empowers the appropriate Government to make rules for carrying out the provisions of the Act, including, among other matters, the threshold of public impact above which a Public Task must be registered under section 3, the form of display of Task information under section 6, the particulars to be proactively published under section 7, the procedure for grievance filing under section 9, the qualifications and terms of office of Accountability Officers and Technical Members under sections 19 and 20, the manner of recovery of the daily penalty under section 27, and the manner of internal registration of Tasks of excluded organisations under section 38. Section 41 makes corresponding provision for rules to be made, in relation to bodies under their own administrative control, by the Speaker, the Chairman, the Chief Justice of India, the Chief Justice of a High Court, the President, the Governor, or the administrator appointed under article 239 of the Constitution, as the case may be.

2. The matters in respect of which rules may be made are matters of procedure and detail, and the delegation of legislative power is of a normal character.
